About Ledger

Ledger is a small, focused tool built around one question: how much do you need to save, and what will it grow into? There's no account to create and no data to hand over — both calculators run entirely in your browser, using the numbers you type in and nothing else.

It exists because most savings math is simple in theory and annoying in practice. Working out a monthly contribution by hand, or checking what a decade of compounding actually does to a balance, means either digging up the formula or trusting a black-box tool. Ledger tries to make that calculation instant and transparent instead.

How the numbers are calculated

Both calculators use the standard future-value-of-annuity formulas used across personal finance: monthly compounding, a constant annual return applied evenly across each month, and — for the Growth Projector — an initial balance that compounds alongside the ongoing contributions. Nothing is rounded until the final display, and a 0% return is handled as a straight division rather than an edge case that breaks the math.

These are projections, not guarantees. Real markets don't return a smooth, constant rate every single month — actual results will be lumpier than the calculator's straight line. Treat the output as a planning estimate, not a promise.
